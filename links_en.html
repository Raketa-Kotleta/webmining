Blockchain for Digital Rights Management

Author: Don Tapscott

This book explores how blockchain can transform digital rights management. The author takes a detailed look at the use of distributed ledgers and smart contracts to automate licensing, royalty payments, and protect content from unauthorized use.

Blockchain Basics: A Non-Technical Introduction in 25 Steps

Daniel Drescher

A simple introduction to the basics of blockchain, written for those without technical experience. The book explains how blockchain works and offers examples of its use, including protecting intellectual property.

Mastering Blockchain: Unlocking the Power of Cryptocurrencies, Smart Contracts, and Decentralized Applications

Author: Imran Bashir

A complete guide to blockchain with a focus on technical details. The author explains how smart contracts and blockchain technologies can be used to create digital rights management systems, making them more transparent and secure.

Blockchain Revolution: How the Technology Behind Bitcoin is Changing Money, Business, and the World

Authors: Don Tapscott, Alex Tapscott

This book takes a deep dive into how blockchain is changing industries, including intellectual property protection. The authors discuss how the technology can create a fair and transparent licensing and payment system for musicians, filmmakers, and other content creators.

Copyright in the Digital Age: Law and Policy

Author: Miguel Peguera

The book is devoted to the legal aspects of copyright management in the digital age. The author analyzes modern problems of content protection on the Internet and considers the use of technologies such as blockchain to improve copyright management systems.
